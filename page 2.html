The Los Angeles Lakers

More details about Minneapolis Lakers history

The Lakers' franchise began in 1947 when Ben Berger and Morris Chalfen(both are from Minnesota) purchased the recently disbanded Detroit Gems of for $15,000 from the owner Maury Winston. Inspired by Minnesota's nickname, "Land of 10,000 Lakes", the team made their new name the Lakers. Sid Hartman, who is a sports write helped the team hire John Kundla to be their first head coach, by meeting with him and selling him on the team. The Lakers had a good roster which included a forward Jim Pollard, playmaker Herm Schaefer, and center George Mikan, who became the most dominant player. In their first season, they had the best record which was 43–17. In 1948, the Lakers moved from the NBL to the BAA, and Mikan's 28.3 point per game (ppg) scoring average set a BAA record. In the 1949 BAA Finals they won the championship, beating the Washington Capitols four games to two. The next season, the team improved their record to 51–17, becoming champions once again. In the 1950–51 season, Mikan won his third straight scoring title at 28.4 ppg and the Lakers had a record of 44–24 to win their second division title. During one of those games, the score was 19–18 and the Lakers were the losing team in that game and the Fort Wayne Pistons being the winners. This game became the lowest scoring game in NBA history. In the playoffs of that season, they defeated the Indianapolis Olympians in three games but unfortunately lost to the Rochester Royals in the next round.

A couple years later...

During their last year in Minneapolis, the Lakers had a record of 25–50. On January 18, 1960, the team was coming off a loss and traveling to St. Louis when their plane crash-landed. Snow storms had took the pilot 150 miles off course when he had to land in a cornfield. Luckily, no one was hurt. Due to their record they got the number two pick in the 1960 NBA draft. During this game, Baylor broke his own record of 64 points. Despite a losing record, the Lakers made the playoffs. The Lakers, however, lost to the Celtics by three points in overtime of game seven.
